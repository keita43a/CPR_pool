Welcome to the Fishery Game Experiment

You and {{ Constants.PLAYERS_PER_GROUP }} participants form a group of {{ Constants.PLAYERS_PER_GROUP }} fishers. Each round, you choose how much effort to exert in harvesting fish.

There are two types of players: Highliners and Lowliners. Highliners are more productive per unit of effort; Lowliners less so. Your role will remain fixed throughout.

Experiment overview

Total rounds: {{ Constants.NUM_ROUNDS }} ({{ Constants.PRACTICE_ROUNDS }} practice, then {{ Constants.OFFICIAL_ROUNDS }} formal)

  • Practice phase: {{ Constants.PRACTICE_ROUNDS }} rounds, in {{ Constants.PRACTICE_ROUNDS }} practice modes (1 round each):
    1. High resource, individual (IND)
    2. Low resource, individual (IND)
    3. High resource, pooling (POOL)
    4. Low resource, pooling (POOL)
  • Formal phase: {{ Constants.OFFICIAL_ROUNDS }} rounds, in 6 modes (7 rounds each):
    1. High resource, individual (IND)
    2. Low resource, individual (IND)
    3. High resource, pooling (POOL)
    4. Low resource, pooling (POOL)
    5. High resource, vote to pool (VOTE)
    6. Low resource, vote to pool (VOTE)

Voting

In the two VOTE modes, you will vote on adopting a pooling system (share total revenue equally) or keeping a decentralized system (each earns based on own harvest). Pooling only occurs if all agree.

Harvesting

Your harvest depends on:

  • Your effort
  • Your productivity (Highliner vs Lowliner)
  • Total group effort (congestion externality)
  • Resource availability (high vs low stock)

Read carefully and ask the experimenter if you have any questions.
